Stromness port

  • Travel
  • Our ferries
  • MV Hamnavoe
View in google map

Port information

Address

Aberdeen Ferry Terminal, Jamieson's Quay, Aberdeen, AB11 5NP, United Kingdom.

Opening hours

Mon - Sun: 05:30-16:45 - 20:00-23:30

Between Monday 31st January 2022 to Sunday 13th February 2022 the Stromness Ferry Terminal will be open from 09:00 to 16:45 (Monday to Sunday) and the Customer Contact Centre will be open from 08:00 to 20:00. (Monday to Sunday).

Check-in

  • Check-in for the 06:30 sailing will open 60 minutes prior to departure. Passengers who have reserved an overnight cabin should check in between 21:30 – 23:30 the previous evening.
  • Check-in for all sailings closes 30 minutes prior to departure.
  • For all other sailings, check-in will open 90 minutes prior to departure. Passengers bringing their car on board are recommended to check at least 1 hour prior to departure.

Parking

  • Car parking is available nearby the Stromness Ferry Terminal, hourly/daily rates apply.
  • Long Stay (free of charge) parking is available at the Ferry Road car park – just north of the Stromness Ferry Terminal – and the Old Academy car park.
  • Short Stay (pay and display) parking is available at the Stromness Ferry Terminal car park and the Ferry Inn car park – opposite the Stromness Ferry Terminal.

Route info

  • Routes: this ferry sails up to 6 times a day with the journey only taking 90 minutes and sailing past the iconic Old Man of Hoy.
  • Connecting port: the ferry sails from Stromness and arrives in Scrabster.
  • Ferries: the MV Hamnavoe sails this route. Find out more about its on board facilitieshere.

Bus, Ferry, and Airport Connections

  • Bus: In Stromness, buses stop opposite the ferry terminal. Buses are operated by Stagecoach. There is a bus service from Stromness which connects with evening ferries to/from Hatston. View theHatston connecting bus information
  • Inter-Island Ferries: Orkney Ferries operate a regular passenger ferry service from Stromness to Graemsay and Hoy and also operate all other inter-island services.
  • Airport: Kirkwall Airport is located 19 miles from Stromness. There is a regular bus service from Stromness to Kirkwall, and from Kirkwall to the airport, operated by Stagecoach. Inter-island services are operated byLoganair

Terminal Facilities

  • Ticket sales and enquiries
  • Tourist information centre
  • Luggage area with lockers
  • Hot and cold drinks vending machines
  • Lift within terminal
  • Disabled access
  • Disabled toilet

Greener travel

  • Electric vehicle charging infrastructure: Visit the Zap-map website or download the app to view electric charge points in the locality.
  • Active Travel: Transport Scotland is committed to increasing levels of cycling and walking for transport and leisure. It goes without saying that walking and cycling benefits an individual’s physical health, but it also provides wider mental health and wellbeing benefits, reduces environmental impacts and helps to keep people’s transport costs down.Learn more
  • Car-sharing: Why not consider sharing your car journeys with people on similar routes / times? This helps to save money and improve our local air quality and wellbeing.
