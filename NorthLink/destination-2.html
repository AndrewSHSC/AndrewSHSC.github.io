Onward, Voyager to the Orkney Islands

The mysteries of the Orkney Islands never fail to attract young and old from far and near. The long-gone voices from the ancient sites dotted around these islands could tell us many tales of ancient civilisations, religious customs, and much more at which we can only guess. The voices may be quiet now, but they have left extraordinary remains of villages, howes, stone circles, and tombs which fire the imagination of all who visit them.

Island history

The mysteries of the Orkney Islands never fail to attract young and old from far and near. Forged by over 5,000 years of history, the Orkney Islands are home to one of Europe’s highest densities of ancient ruins – an average of three archaeological sites per square mile. From Neolithic settlements to imposing stone circles, the ancient and enchanting Orkney Islands make for an essential visit for history lovers.

Scenery and wildlife

With rolling fields bordered by huge seascapes, picturesque white sandy beaches, and impressive sea stacks – Orkney’s scenery is truly breath-taking. Wander along towering sea cliffs, and witness the sound and sight of thousands of seabirds who soar through the sky. The pristine waters surrounding the isles host a spectacular range of marine life too, with sightings of orca, whales and dolphins seen all year-round.

Orkney Shops, Accommodation and Eating Out

Orkney has a range of accommodation including self-catering cottages, campsites and cosy local hotels to choose from. Across 17 inhabited islands, it’s easy to find the best local cuisine, including hand-crafted gin, fresh meat, baked goods and smoked cheeses to savour. Explore these enchanting islands on foot, by bike or rent a car for a leisurely spot of sight-seeing.
